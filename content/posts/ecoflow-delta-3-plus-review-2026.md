# EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus Review: The Smartest 1kWh Power Station We’ve Tested

**Last Updated: May 27, 2026**

EcoFlow has two products with confusingly similar names: the DELTA 3 Plus ($649, 1,024 Wh) and the DELTA Pro 3 ($1,999, 4,096 Wh). This review is about the smaller one — the DELTA 3 Plus — and why it might be the better buy for most people.

At $649, the DELTA 3 Plus delivers 1,024 Wh of LiFePO4 capacity, 1,800 W of continuous AC output, and a 56-minute wall charge. Those specs are competitive on their own, but what sets this unit apart is the software. The EcoFlow app is the most capable power-station companion app on the market, with smart-home integration, scheduled charging, quiet mode, and over-the-air firmware updates that actually add features months after purchase.

We ran the DELTA 3 Plus through a 72-hour home-backup simulation, smart-home integration tests, solar charging, and side-by-side noise comparisons. Here is what the data says.

## Noise & Thermal Behavior

We tested noise output at three load levels using a calibrated sound meter at 1 meter distance in a 22 °C room.

| Load Level | Power Draw | Noise Level | Real-World Comparison |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Idle / no load** | 0 W | **25.3 dB** | Barely audible |
| **Medium load** | 500 W | **35.8 dB** | Quiet office; non-intrusive |
| **Full load** | 1,800 W | **42.1 dB** | Soft conversation level |
| **Quiet mode, 500 W** | 500 W | **29.7 dB** | Bedroom-safe; fan limited |

The quiet mode is genuinely useful. By limiting fan speed in the app, you trade ~15 % charging speed for a 6 dB noise reduction. At 29.7 dB, the DELTA 3 Plus in quiet mode is comparable to the Jackery 2000 v2 at idle (24.1 dB) — impressive for a actively cooled unit.

Thermal imaging after a 30-minute 1,800 W load test showed a maximum surface temperature of 42 °C (108 °F) at the exhaust vent. The chassis stayed below 35 °C everywhere else. Heat dissipation is well-managed.

## Who Should Buy It

**Buy the EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus if:**
- You want the best app and smart-home integration in portable power
- Fast charging (56 minutes) matters more than maximum capacity
- You run 4–5 devices simultaneously and need the extra AC outlet
- You use Alexa, Google Assistant, or Apple HomeKit and want voice control
- You want scheduled charging for time-of-use electricity rates
- You need a 1 kWh unit that can still handle a 1,500 W space heater in an emergency

**Skip it if:**
- You need 2 kWh for overnight backup (Jackery 2000 v2 is the pick)
- Noise under 30 dB is non-negotiable (Jackery wins, even without quiet mode)
- You want the absolute fastest charging (Anker C1000 Gen 2 at 49 minutes)
- You plan to expand capacity later (DELTA Pro 3 is expandable; this is not)
- You are price-sensitive and can catch the Anker C1000 Gen 2 on sale at $470

## Final Verdict

The EcoFlow DELTA 3 Plus is the smartest 1 kWh power station on the market. It does not have the biggest battery or the quietest operation, but it has the best software, the most useful app features, and genuine smart-home integration that no competitor matches.

At $649, it is a fair price for what you get — especially if you value the convenience of scheduled charging, voice control, and firmware updates that add features over time. For tech-forward buyers who want a power station that feels like a modern smart device, the DELTA 3 Plus is the obvious choice.
