## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between a power bank and a portable power station?
A power bank delivers DC power only (via USB), typically up to 100W. A portable power station includes an inverter to supply AC power (household outlets), plus larger batteries (≥288Wh) and solar inputs. They're different tool categories — not just size variants.

### How long will a 1000Wh power station run my refrigerator?
A typical refrigerator uses 100-200W when running (compressor cycling on/off). A 1000Wh station will run it for 5-10 hours of continuous use, or 12-24 hours of real-world use with the compressor cycling. Get 2000Wh+ for overnight fridge backup.

### Are LiFePO4 batteries worth the premium?
**Yes.** In 2026, LiFePO4 is the standard for quality power stations. They last 3,000-6,000+ cycles (vs. 500-800 for older Li-NMC), are safer (lower fire risk), and perform better in temperature extremes. All our picks use LiFePO4.

### Can I use a power station while it's charging?
**Yes.** This is called pass-through charging. All models in this guide support it, and the Anker, Jackery, and ALLWEI also offer UPS mode — instant switchover if grid power cuts out.

### Do I need solar panels?
Not necessarily. All these stations charge from wall outlets. But solar panels make sense for off-grid camping, RV life, or extended outages. They're the only way to recharge when the grid is down for days.
